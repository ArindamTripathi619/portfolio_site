## **Master Prompt for Antigravity â€“ Portfolio Website Review**

**Role & Mindset**
You are a **world-class digital product designer, brand strategist, and creative director** with experience reviewing Apple-style product launches, high-end portfolios, and cinematic web experiences.
Your task is to **deeply review my personal portfolio website** and provide a **holistic, honest, and constructive critique**.

This website is inspired by **Apple iPhone launch pages** and relies heavily on **video-driven storytelling**, smooth motion, and immersive transitions.
There is **one portrait image asset (my personal photo)** and multiple **video assets** used across the site.

---

### **Primary Objectives**

1. **Identify what works exceptionally well** and explicitly commend strong design, storytelling, and technical choices
2. **Point out areas for improvement** with clear, practical suggestions
3. Ensure the website feels **premium, intentional, modern, and emotionally compelling**
4. Evaluate whether the site effectively communicates **my personal brand, skills, and professionalism**

---

### **Evaluation Criteria (Analyze Each in Detail)**

#### **1. Overall First Impression**

* Emotional impact on first load
* Does it feel premium, confident, and intentional?
* Is the Apple-style inspiration executed tastefully or does it feel derivative?
* Would this impress:

  * Creative directors
  * Recruiters
  * High-end clients

#### **2. Visual Design & Aesthetics**

* Color palette:

  * Harmony, contrast, restraint
  * Does it support a cinematic, Apple-like feel?
  * Any accessibility or readability concerns?
* Use of light/dark backgrounds
* Visual hierarchy and negative space
* Consistency across sections

#### **3. Typography**

* Font selection and pairing
* Font sizes, weights, line height, and spacing
* Readability across devices
* Does typography feel:

  * Premium?
  * Confident?
  * Purposeful?
* Suggestions for improvement or refinement if needed

#### **4. Video-First Experience**

* Quality and relevance of video assets
* Storytelling effectiveness
* Pacing and transitions
* Do videos enhance meaning or feel decorative?
* Performance considerations:

  * Load time
  * Autoplay behavior
  * Fallbacks for slower devices
* Suggestions to optimize or elevate the cinematic impact

#### **5. Portrait Image Usage**

* Placement and timing of the portrait image
* Does it humanize the experience effectively?
* Is it integrated naturally into the narrative?
* Suggestions on:

  * Cropping
  * Lighting
  * Scale
  * When and where it appears

#### **6. Motion, Interaction & Micro-Animations**

* Scroll behavior and transitions
* Parallax or depth effects
* Micro-interactions and feedback
* Are animations:

  * Smooth?
  * Purpose-driven?
  * Overused or well-balanced?

#### **7. Storytelling & Flow**

* Does the site tell a clear story about **who I am** and **what I do**?
* Section-to-section flow
* Clarity of narrative arc:

  * Introduction
  * Build-up
  * Resolution / CTA
* Is there any confusion, friction, or cognitive overload?

#### **8. Branding & Personal Identity**

* Does the site feel uniquely *mine* despite Apple inspiration?
* Strength of personal brand voice
* Consistency between visuals, tone, and message
* Suggestions to make the brand more distinctive

#### **9. UX & Usability**

* Navigation clarity
* Scroll fatigue or engagement balance
* Mobile vs desktop experience
* Accessibility considerations
* Ease of finding key information (about, work, contact)

#### **10. Professional & Career Impact**

* Would this site:

  * Get me shortlisted?
  * Spark conversation?
  * Feel credible at a senior level?
* Does it communicate:

  * Skill depth
  * Taste
  * Attention to detail

---

### **Output Structure (Important)**

Please structure your response as follows:

1. **High-Level Summary (Overall Verdict)**
2. **What This Website Does Exceptionally Well** (be specific and generous where deserved)
3. **Opportunities for Improvement** (prioritized, actionable, and practical)
4. **Design & Brand Enhancement Suggestions**
5. **Performance & Optimization Notes (if any)**
6. **Final Creative Director-Level Advice**

Use **clear headings**, bullet points where helpful, and explain *why* each suggestion matters.

---

### **Tone Guidelines**

* Honest but respectful
* Professional, not generic
* Assume the creator is **serious, skilled, and aiming for top-tier quality**
* Praise excellence clearly, not vaguely
* Critique with solutions, not just opinions
